1

Build Your AI Job-Finding Engine

Use AI to find the right opportunities intelligently. Build an agent that scans job boards, growth signals, and hiring announcements so you can focus on the roles that actually fit.

Deliverable: A working AI agent that sources opportunities daily

Learning Objectives

  • Define personal role-fit criteria across function, culture, growth, and compensation dimensions
  • Use AI to batch-analyze job postings and identify high-match opportunities
  • Build a reusable AI agent/prompt for daily job matching
  • Create a curated shortlist of 10 target roles with rationale

Pre-Work

~40 min total

Reflect on your career criteria 15 min

Write down what matters most across four dimensions: function (what work you want to do), culture (what environment you thrive in), growth (where you want to be in 2-3 years), and compensation (your floor and target).

Gather 5-10 job postings you find interesting 20 min

Browse LinkedIn, Indeed, or your preferred job board. Save postings that caught your eye, even if they're not a perfect fit. We'll use these as raw material for AI analysis.

Set up a free AI tool 5 min

Create an account on ChatGPT or Claude (free tier) if you don't already have one. No paid subscription needed. Your $20 AI credit covers usage for agent-building sessions.

90-Minute Agenda

5 min

Welcome & Introductions

Meet your cohort, bootcamp overview, what you'll walk away with today.

10 min

The Strategy Problem

Why spray-and-pray fails. 59% of job seekers search 3+ months. The shift from "fix my resume" to "fix my strategy."

15 min

Role-Fit Framework

Four dimensions: function fit, culture fit, growth trajectory, compensation. You'll fill in your own criteria worksheet.

20 min

Live AI Demo: Job Matching

Instructor feeds 50+ job postings into AI with role-fit criteria. Walk through prompt engineering. Surface top 5 matches and explain why each scored high.

25 min

Hands-On Workshop

Build your own AI job matching prompt. Test with real job boards. Refine criteria in real time. Peer feedback in breakout rooms.

10 min

Building Your Daily Agent

Set up your prompt to run daily: saved prompts, custom GPTs, automation options. Demo of scheduling.

5 min

Wrap-Up & Preview

Review deliverable checklist. Preview Session 2. Between-session assignments.

Homework

~1.5 hrs over the week

Run your AI matching agent daily for one week 10 min/day

Paste your prompt into ChatGPT or Claude each day with fresh job postings. Note which roles score highest and why.

Refine your role-fit criteria 15 min

After a few days, adjust your criteria. Too many matches? Tighten your requirements. Too few? Broaden one dimension.

Finalize your top 10 target roles 20 min

Lock in your shortlist of 10 roles and identify your top 3 dream companies. Research 5 people at these companies you'd like to connect with.

Next session: Bring your finalized top 10 roles and a list of 5 people at target companies to Session 2, where you'll craft outreach messages and practice networking conversations.
2

Turn Cold Outreach into Warm Conversations

Learn who to connect with, how to write messages that get replies, and how to have conversations that lead to real next steps.

Deliverable: 5 real outreach messages sent + 1 live role-play

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the right people to contact at your target companies (peers, hiring managers, connectors)
  • Write personalized outreach messages that get responses, not ignores
  • Use AI to research people and personalize at scale without sounding robotic
  • Practice informational interview conversations through live role-play

Pre-Work

~40 min total

Research 5 people from your target companies 25 min

For each of the 5 people you identified in Session 1 homework, spend a few minutes: read their recent LinkedIn posts, note their career path, find something specific you can reference. The best outreach messages reference something real.

Write one outreach message on your own (no AI) 10 min

Before we teach the AI-assisted approach, write a genuine connection request to one of your targets. We'll compare your natural voice to AI-generated versions in session.

Read: The anatomy of a great cold message 5 min

Read the provided 3-minute guide on what makes outreach messages get responses vs. get ignored. Focus on the framework: specific hook, clear ask, easy out.

90-Minute Agenda

5 min

Check-In

How did your AI agent perform this week? Quick wins and surprises from daily matching. Any standout roles or companies?

10 min

The Networking Mindset Shift

Why networking isn't "using people." The informational interview as a genuine learning conversation. How 70% of jobs are filled through connections, not applications.

15 min

Live AI Demo: Personalized Outreach

Take 3 real profiles from a volunteer's list. Use AI to generate personalized connection messages. Compare AI-only vs. AI-assisted (your research + AI writing). Show the quality difference.

25 min

Hands-On: Craft & Send Your Messages

Write 5 personalized outreach messages using the AI-assisted method. Each must reference something specific about the recipient. Peer review: would you respond to this? Then hit send.

20 min

Informational Interview Masterclass + Live Role-Play

The 5-question framework that gets referrals. How to ask without asking. Role-play practice in pairs with live feedback. AI-generated follow-up templates for after the call.

15 min

Wrap-Up

Deliverable check: 5 messages sent, role-play completed. Preview Session 3. Homework assignments.

Homework

~1.5 hrs over the week

Send 5 more outreach messages 20 min

Expand your outreach to 10 total. Track who responds and who doesn't. Note patterns in what works.

Conduct 1-2 informational interviews 1 hr

If any connections respond positively, schedule and complete at least one informational interview using the 5-question framework. Send a follow-up within 24 hours.

Audit your LinkedIn profile 10 min

Screenshot your current LinkedIn headline, summary, and experience section. Note what feels outdated or generic. Bring these screenshots to Session 3.

Next session: Bring your LinkedIn audit screenshots and your current resume to Session 3, where you'll optimize both for ATS algorithms and human readers.
3

Beat the Bots, Impress the Humans

Optimize your resume and LinkedIn for both ATS algorithms and human readers. Find your authentic posting topics and build your personal brand.

Deliverable: Refined resume, LinkedIn profile, and a drafted post

Learning Objectives

  • Understand how ATS systems actually filter applications (and debunk the myths)
  • Use AI to perform a gap analysis between your resume and target job postings
  • Optimize your LinkedIn headline, summary, and skills section for recruiter visibility
  • Develop a content strategy and publish your first brand-building LinkedIn post

Pre-Work

~30 min total

Pick your #1 target role and copy the full posting 10 min

From your top 10 list, choose the single role you'd most like to apply to. Copy the full job posting text into a document. We'll use this for live resume optimization.

Prepare your current resume 5 min

Have your resume ready as a Google Doc or Word file (not PDF). We'll be making real edits during the session, so you need an editable format.

Find 3 LinkedIn profiles you admire 15 min

Look at people in roles similar to your targets. What makes their profiles compelling? Note specific headlines, summary structures, or featured sections that stand out.

90-Minute Agenda

5 min

Check-In

Networking wins from the week. Any responses, informational interviews, or surprising connections?

10 min

ATS Demystified

How ATS systems actually parse resumes. What gets filtered and why. The 80/20 of what recruiters actually scan (data-backed, including the 6-second scan research).

15 min

Live AI Demo: Resume Gap Analysis

Feed a job posting + sample resume into AI. Get a detailed gap analysis: missing keywords, weak bullet points, formatting issues. Walk through the prompt.

20 min

Hands-On: Optimize Your Resume

Run your own resume through AI gap analysis against your target posting. Rewrite top bullet points live. Peer review in breakout rooms. Instructor circulates for feedback.

15 min

LinkedIn Profile Rebuild

AI-powered audit of your headline (the most-scanned element). Summary rewrite for target roles. Skills section alignment with ATS keywords. Before/after examples.

15 min

Content Strategy: Draft & Publish

Why posting matters for job seekers. 3 post formats that work: insight, story, question. Use AI to draft your first post from a real work experience. Hit publish live.

10 min

Wrap-Up

Deliverable check: resume optimized, LinkedIn refreshed, post published. Preview Session 4. Homework assignments.

Homework

~1 hr 15 min

Finalize your ATS-optimized resume 30 min

Incorporate all feedback. Run one final AI gap analysis to verify keyword coverage. Save as both editable format and PDF.

Complete your full LinkedIn profile update 25 min

Finish all sections: headline, summary, experience (top 3 roles), skills (reordered for target role), and featured section. Write and publish a second LinkedIn post.

Brainstorm your AI project idea 20 min

Think about a workflow in your field that could be automated or improved with AI. Write a 2-3 sentence description of the problem and how AI could help. Bring this to Session 4.

Next session: Bring your AI project idea and your laptop set up with Claude Code or ChatGPT to Session 4, where you'll build a portfolio-worthy AI project.
4

Build an AI Project That Gets You Hired

Build an automated workflow using Claude Code to demonstrate AI-native skills. Create a portfolio piece that shows what you can do, and learn to talk about it in interviews.

Deliverable: A portfolio AI project for GitHub and LinkedIn

Learning Objectives

  • Scope an AI project that demonstrates practical business value, not just technical skill
  • Build a working automated workflow using Claude Code or similar AI tools
  • Document and present your project for a GitHub portfolio and LinkedIn showcase
  • Craft the "tell me about a time you used AI" interview answer using your project

Pre-Work

~35 min total

Refine your AI project idea 15 min

Take the idea you brainstormed and flesh it out: What problem does it solve? Who benefits? What does the input look like? What does the output look like? Think about problems in your industry or daily work that involve repetitive information processing.

Set up your tools 10 min

Ensure Claude Code or ChatGPT is ready. If you want to publish to GitHub, create a free account at github.com. No coding experience required; we'll walk through everything step by step.

Browse 3 example AI projects 10 min

Review the provided examples of simple but impressive AI projects: email classifiers, report generators, data summarizers. Note what makes each one compelling as a portfolio piece.

90-Minute Agenda

5 min

Check-In

Resume and LinkedIn progress. Post engagement updates. Any callbacks or interviews from optimized applications?

10 min

Why AI Projects Win Interviews

What hiring managers are actually looking for when they say "AI experience." The difference between using AI and building with AI. How to scope a project that's impressive but achievable in 60 minutes.

15 min

Live Demo: Building an AI Workflow

Instructor builds a complete AI-automated workflow live from scratch using Claude Code. Walk through: defining the problem, writing the prompt, testing, iterating, and documenting. Template project structure provided.

35 min

Hands-On: Build Your Project

Build your own AI project using the guided template. Instructor and TAs circulate for help. Work in pairs for peer support. Get a working prototype by the end of this block.

15 min

Document & Showcase

Write a compelling project README. Post your project to GitHub (guided walkthrough). Draft a LinkedIn post about what you built and why. Frame your project for the interview question: "Tell me about a time you used AI."

10 min

Wrap-Up

Deliverable check: working project, GitHub repo, LinkedIn draft. Preview Session 5. Homework assignments.

Homework

~1 hr 15 min

Polish and publish your AI project 30 min

Refine your project based on session feedback. Finalize the GitHub README with screenshots or examples. Publish your LinkedIn post about the project.

List 5 work accomplishments you're proud of 20 min

Think about times you solved a hard problem, led a project, improved a process, or handled a difficult situation. Write 1-2 sentences about each. These become raw material for your behavioral interview answers.

Draft your "tell me about yourself" answer 25 min

Write a 60-90 second script that covers: where you've been, what you're great at, and why you're excited about what's next. Don't overthink it; we'll refine it in Session 5.

Next session: Bring your 5 accomplishments and "tell me about yourself" draft to Session 5, where you'll craft polished behavioral interview answers and practice with live feedback.
5

Own Your Story: Behavioral Interview Excellence

Craft the "tell me about yourself" answer that opens doors. Practice the intangibles: energy, specificity, and authentic presence.

Deliverable: A polished "tell me about yourself" script

Learning Objectives

  • Craft a compelling "tell me about yourself" answer that sets the tone for any interview
  • Transform work accomplishments into polished STAR-method behavioral answers
  • Practice the intangibles that interviewers actually evaluate: energy, specificity, and presence
  • Build a reusable answer bank for the most common behavioral interview questions

Pre-Work

~35 min total

Review your 5 accomplishments 10 min

Re-read the accomplishments you listed in homework. For each one, note: What was the situation? What did you specifically do? What was the measurable result? We'll polish these into STAR answers.

Practice your "tell me about yourself" draft out loud 10 min

Read your draft aloud 3 times. Time yourself. Note where you stumble or where it feels scripted. Record yourself on your phone if possible. We'll workshop this in session.

Watch: Strong vs. weak behavioral answers 15 min

Watch the provided video showing examples of strong vs. weak behavioral interview responses. Note the difference between vague answers and specific, structured ones. Pay attention to energy and delivery, not just content.

90-Minute Agenda

5 min

Check-In

AI project showcase: anyone publish their project this week? LinkedIn engagement updates. Interview pipeline check.

15 min

"Tell Me About Yourself" Workshop

The 3-part formula: past, present, future. Live rewriting of volunteer scripts. What makes an answer memorable vs. forgettable. The 60-90 second sweet spot.

20 min

Hands-On: Polish Your Script

Rewrite your "tell me about yourself" using AI and the 3-part formula. Practice in pairs with live feedback. Focus on energy and authenticity, not perfection.

15 min

STAR Method Deep Dive

Transform your 5 accomplishments into polished STAR answers using AI. Instructor demos with a volunteer's real accomplishment. The difference between "I was part of" and "I led."

20 min

Mock Interview Rounds

Live mock behavioral interviews in small groups. Each person answers 2 questions with real-time feedback. Practice handling curveball questions: "Why are you leaving?" and salary questions.

15 min

Wrap-Up

Deliverable check: polished script ready. Preview Session 6 (assessments and capstone). Homework assignments.

Homework

~1 hr 30 min

Complete 2 full mock interviews with AI 45 min

Use the AI mock interview prompt from the session. Do one behavioral interview and one role-specific interview for your #1 target. Record yourself if possible and review.

Expand your STAR answer bank to 8-10 answers 30 min

Cover the essentials: leadership, conflict, failure, teamwork, initiative, problem-solving, using AI. These should feel natural, not memorized.

Research a practice assessment for your target role 15 min

Find an example of the kind of assessment your target roles use: case study, take-home project, live coding challenge, or presentation. Bring this to Session 6.

Next session: Bring your practice assessment example and your complete interview prep materials to Session 6, where you'll master assessments and assemble your complete job search playbook.

Master the universal framework for any skills assessment: take-home projects, case interviews, live challenges, and presentations.

Deliverable: A completed practice assessment + reusable framework

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the different assessment types and what each one is really evaluating
  • Apply a universal framework for approaching any skills assessment with confidence
  • Use AI strategically during assessments (when it helps vs. when it hurts)
  • Complete a timed practice assessment and get peer feedback

Pre-Work

~30 min total

Review your practice assessment example 10 min

Re-read the assessment you found as homework. Identify: What skills is this testing? What does a great answer look like? Where would you get stuck?

Reflect on your bootcamp journey 10 min

Review what you've built across Sessions 1-5: AI matching agent, outreach messages sent, optimized resume, LinkedIn profile, AI portfolio project, behavioral answers. Note any gaps you want to strengthen.

Write your biggest remaining job search concern 10 min

What's the one thing that still makes you nervous? Assessment anxiety? Salary negotiation? Imposter syndrome? We'll address the top concerns in session.

5 min

Check-In

15 min

The Assessment Landscape

The Universal Framework

The 5-step approach that works for any assessment: Clarify, Structure, Execute, Polish, Present. Live walkthrough of the framework applied to a sample case study.

25 min

Timed Practice Assessment

10 min

Assemble Your Complete Playbook

10 min

Celebration & Next Steps

Share one commitment with the group. 30-day challenge kickoff. Alumni community introduction. Metrics to track. Final Q&A.

Block time on your calendar for your first full week following the system. Commit to running it for 30 days.
